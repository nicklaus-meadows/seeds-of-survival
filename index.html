Growing Security for a Growing World.

Seeds of Survival

Seed of Survival is an exhibition that aims to inform the community about our food, where it comes from, the impact transportation has on the earth, and the vulnerabilities we set ourselves up for relying on brittle supply chains. We hope to offer knowledge, support, and education on how everyone can make a positive impact on the earth we all share but also protect their families at the same time.

Through the exhibit we can learn about our own personal impacts on the environment and how to make a change for a better future.

Key Takeaways

  • Learn About Food Miles
  • Learn About Carbon Footprint
  • Learn About Local Food
  • Learn About Growing Your Own Food
  • Learn About Preserving Your Own Food

Food Miles.

What Are they? How do We Reduce them?

Food miles refer to the distance food travels from where it is produced to where it is consumed. The more miles food travels, the more carbon emissions are generated from transportation, contributing to climate change. To reduce food miles we can make a conscious effort to learn and care about where our food comes from exactly.

A Japanese study found that a family eating locally grown food could reduce carbon emissions by 300 Kilograms annually. That is equivalent to taking a car off the road for a few months. While this impact alone might seem small, if everyone participated 1,000s of car CO2 could be removed from our atmosphere.

Source: Halweil, Brian. 2006. Food Democracy: Nourishing a Fundamental Freedom. Heifer International World Ark. Jan/Feb 2006: 6-13.

Going Local

While always a staple in the food movement, local food co-ops and farmers markets have seen a rise in popularity around the country. Many people are know taking a deeper look at where our food comes from. A lot of the interest can be attributed to people wanting to eat healthier, without the overly processed food on a supermarket shelf. Many want to lowers their carbon foot print to hopefully provide a better world for the next generations. Some just enjoy getting out of the house and enjoying the community. We encourage everyone to find a local farmers market or farm to table co-op and experience all that it has to offer.

The Quantity of Farmer's markets have seen a significant increase in the US. In 1994 there were roughly 1,500 farmers markets in the nation. As of 2019 that number is almost eclipsing 9,000 according to the FDA.
